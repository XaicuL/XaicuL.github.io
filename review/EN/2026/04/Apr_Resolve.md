
Passing over the days of March, with a name tag called April, running as a Next Runner

Looking back at the calendar, March was incredibly dense, filled with many experiences and emotions I've never felt before in my life.
Sometimes these feelings felt so precious, but I realized I shouldn't hold onto them forever.
So, I've archived them in my diary and decided to run forward as a "Next Runner."

My goal for April is just one, since a grand goal might scare my brain:
**"To prepare for my first university exams so that no regret remains."**
If I can achieve just this, I have nothing more to wish for. Truly, as long as there is no regret.

But lately—even up until just now—one question has been circling in my mind.
This wasn't a question I just pulled out of thin air; here's the context:
From around late March, some of my department peers suddenly approached me, saying they wanted to get closer.

I... honestly don't know why. I've just been busy sitting in the front row for every class and studying in my dorm room. I still don't understand what part of me made them want to be friends. ~~Maybe I'm just being over-analytical; they're probably just being friendly peers.~~

But one question derived from this is:
**"What kind of person should I be remembered as?"**

To be honest, this is one of the questions I hate most. It reminds me of my high school days.
I wanted to meet people as just "Hyunjun Jeon" without wearing a mask, to be remembered as just "Hyunjun Jeon," and to mingle as "Hyunjun Jeon."
The fact that this question is even arising feels like proof that I'm still meeting people while wearing a mask, so I've personally tried to avoid it.

But lately, I've been pondering this question more deeply.
Looking back at my hellish high school life, in the 1st year, I talked a lot to my peers about what I wanted to do, but I didn't tell the teachers. My life back then was somewhat okay.
But from the 2nd year, when I started saying "I'm going to do this!", I faced all sorts of pressure and persecution. Since the current situation feels similar to that time, and I never want to go back there, it feels like I'm retracing those steps.

So, I'm thinking deeply about that question these days.

Changing topics—

If you ask me what I fear most in April, it's nothing other than the exams.
Since I don't know the difficulty or the style of the midterms, it feels like the "Final Boss."
I've judged that it's best to start preparing at least two weeks in advance.
Even if the content seems easy, since it's my first midterm, starting just a week before carries too much risk if my predictions are wrong.
So, at least two weeks. ~~Of course, if I find two weeks is too long after trying it, I can adjust it to one week next time.~~

I think April will just whisk by as I do this.
But if you ask if April is unimportant because of that? **Absolutely not.**

Just opening my GoodNotes right now, there are so many things I need to change in my V3 monograph. No matter how much I mark it, more things keep appearing every time I look.
~~Of course, there are **plans** for a V4... and maybe even a V5.~~

If I decide to go up to V5, I'll stop public releases at V4 and think about presenting V5 after a formal submission.
If V3 feels sufficient, I might not submit V4 to ArXiv but instead submit it directly to a conference for presentation.

If someone reads my papers on ArXiv, my greatest wish is that they read from V1 through V3 and feel a sense of thrill. It felt vaguely like writing a **Divine Comedy** (or a New Song - Shingok).
~~Sorry, I'm overreacting again.~~
Like Goethe's **Faust**— ~~No, that's enough. I overreacted.~~

Actually, it might seem like I put everything into V3, but I didn't include the truly important things, and there have been massive changes since then.
Of course, "change" doesn't necessarily mean **removal or replacement**; the fact that it's an **addition** is very positive.

I don't know if any reader has seen V3, but I deliberately didn't reveal the weights or the core logic, which are the most essential parts. I was a bit embarrassed, and it wasn't finished yet.
But after releasing V3 on ArXiv, even more have been added: **Binary Conversations**, **New Symbols**, **Three-Body**.

It probably won't make sense to you, and it shouldn't.
~~If it does make sense, please email me with your address and the time you're home alone. I'll send you a gift.~~
Just kidding. If it makes sense, I want to fly together.

Well... even as I'm writing this, I still haven't found the answer to what kind of person I should be remembered as. I'm not a kind person. But I'm not someone who treats people poorly either.
I'm not someone who never gets angry. On the other hand, I don't get angry often.

I don't know. I don't know what to do.
What kind of person should I remain as? The answer to that question usually comes when regret follows.
Maybe I'm still not an adult despite getting older. I still haven't decided what kind of person I should be or how I should appear to others.

Honestly, I feel like a child with grand dreams but tiny actions—like a baby who has learned Taekwondo for two months shouting "TAE!! KWON!!" while throwing a punch at me.

It's just a cycle of "Should I do this?" -> Add -> If it fails, rename and release a more perfect version -> "Should I do this?" -> If it doesn't fail, "Wow, this is it!" -> Try to add more -> Repeat repeat repeat.

I learn a lot in that process and have many points to ponder.
I think I'll submit it somewhere within this year. Even with a T4... surely... haha...

Okay, let's end this. It's getting too long.
For April, nothing else is more important than this:
**"Make the set of regrets an empty set,"** and **"Protect myself."**
If I can keep these two, I'm determined that I won't have regrets even if I look back at this time next year.

I want to write a phrase I love lately:
