
Pausing the steps as a "Next Runner," contemplating the passing April.

... April truly flew by. No joke, it felt like only about two weeks had passed.
Looking back at my emotions in April, if I were to plot it on a graph, it would follow the pattern of $y = (x-1)(x-2)(x-3)$.

First of all, I experienced my first "group project" collaboration at university.
Since there were moments of conflicting opinions, looking back now, there were many lessons to learn and parts of myself to reflect on.

Also... I took my first university "midterm" exams.
While there were many things to learn from that, I'm more delighted by the fact that I now understand and can anticipate about 80% of what happens in university life.

But before that, let's look back at my "day job."
What did I accomplish? It's a bit disappointing that I have to distinguish between what can and cannot be disclosed here, but then again, I wonder, "Who's actually reading this?"
Regardless, I myself am greatly looking forward to the version following V3 of the LNNet research.

After releasing V3 on ArXiv, I naturally spent a lot of time on the KTX between university and home, rereading my own paper as if it were written by a third party.
"Why did I make it this hard to read?" "Why didn't I notice this when I was writing?"
I marked many parts with checkmarks and devoted a significant amount of time to planning the next version.

In that process of pouring in time, a thought occurred to me. I asked myself: **"What is the core Focus Point of my research?"**
Naturally, I thought it was being an **Active Discoverer**. Upon reaching that answer and looking back at my V3, I realized that the core of my research was being handled rather weakly.

Of course, I've already planned everything from V2 to V3, V4, and subsequent research.
Yet, I still find myself pondering before sleep: "Why did I plan to move content that could have been revealed in V3 into V4 instead?" ~~It sounds like a lie, but it's true...~~

Anyway, back to university life. April had days of great joy, but also days where I couldn't sleep out of pure anger.
I'll only introduce the joyful days. It's a waste of my fingers to describe the angry ones.
Since I ended up just smiling while others were acting like "Angry Birds," there's no need for me to describe it here.

The joyful part is... maybe, just maybe, I might be able to join the lab of the advisor I truly respect this summer.
I remember it was right before midterms. I met the professor on my way to the restroom.
The professor followed me in, and while we were taking care of business, he asked me: **"What are you doing this summer?"**
I replied: **"Uh... I'll probably be doing some research... studying... studying some more, then working on a paper... and then the second semester will start, right?"**
In the subsequent 5-minute conversation, he made something close to an offer for an undergraduate research internship. We decided to talk more after midterms.

~~At that time, I didn't know that hearing those words would turn into fear for me...~~

After that, I naturally pushed myself even harder for midterms. I think I only slept for 5 hours over 3 days.
But a bottleneck occurred... At some point, I wasn't striving for good grades for the sake of it, but rather running away from the fear that poor grades would lead to the withdrawal of the internship offer and disappointment from my parents. I became a person grinding my own body down.

I knew it in my heart.
A part of me thought, "If he's the type of person to withdraw the offer based on midterm scores, he would have waited until after midterms to ask."
But for some reason, another part of me kept thinking, **"If I mess up midterms, he'll take it back."** And so I pushed myself to the limit.

Then came the dawn of the first exam day... I still remember it was 5:17 AM.
Suddenly, my body felt strange. It felt just like the day three days before the CSAT when I had the flu but didn't know it yet and kept pushing myself.
At first, I just thought I was tired, so I took a glucose candy and a caffeine pill.
And just in case of caffeine side effects, I also took some L-theanine.

Something was wrong. My body sent even stranger signals.
I instinctively avoided coffee, and my body just wanted to lie down...
It felt so strange. I wondered if I had taken a GABA pill instead of caffeine.
Or maybe it was because I had eaten a glucose candy 5 minutes prior and then another one, causing a sugar spike?

No... it was neither. Thinking by process of elimination, the only thing left was being sick.
I really didn't want to admit it at that moment. This was the exam for the professor who had just given me an offer; failing it felt like I would hate myself too much.

I tried to endure it somehow... but in the end, I lost.
I couldn't even take the exam and went to the hospital. I don't remember much, but the doctor gave me what could be called "advice" in a nice way, or... ~~"scolding" in a not-so-nice way.~~

The whole time I was in the hospital, I hated myself.
I hated myself so much that all sorts of thoughts went through my head.

However, when I called my advisor to explain the situation, he actually laughed and advised me to practice applying the "law of diminishing returns" to my own life.
No one knows that I stood outside the hospital for 5 minutes with tears in my eyes after that call.

Anyway, back to the story... after midterms ended, I had about a 30-minute discussion with the professor.
We tentatively agreed that I would start this summer vacation, once the seniors have time to hand over their tasks.

And what else... Oh, this overlaps with May, but I'm currently working on moving the dataset to Hugging Face again.
I'll talk more about this in May.

April... Wow... Writing it down like this, it seems like nothing special, yet it flew by so fast.
On the other hand, it feels a bit bittersweet that I'll never again feel the tension of the very first university midterm in my life.

My current dilemma is whether to reveal V4 with a "Bang!" at a formal conference, or to release V4 on ArXiv and then reveal V5 with a "Bang!" at a conference.

Looking at the current pace, honestly, I think I'll have to put V4 on ArXiv.
I thought I had included quite a bit in V3, but looking back, V3 feels more like V1+V2+V3(partial), almost like a post just bragging about how I made the dataset. V4... actually, that's what V3 should have been.
Anyway, I'm working on V4, but I can't include everything.
I'm deliberately excluding some parts, pursuing the aesthetics of restraint. ~~Not that anyone's watching anyway!!!~~

It's a feeling of being reminded once again of how great Dante's "Divine Comedy" and Goethe's "Faust" are.

Let's finish now.
The plans for LNNet research are overflowing.
And even if it's not under the name LNNet, there is other research I want to do.
No one knows if my research is good or just the delusions of a sick dreamer.

However, everyone knows that I still have many OCWs left to listen to... haha;;

April.
The month when flowers are in full bloom, bees are flying, the wind is blowing, and it's perfect for making memories.
This is how I spent my April. Thinking of others' Aprils brings a smile to my face, and makes me want to offer comfort.
Yet, in the passing time, I continue to run steadily, and I intend to throw myself once again into the approaching future.

That's how I end this. Goodbye! bye :)
