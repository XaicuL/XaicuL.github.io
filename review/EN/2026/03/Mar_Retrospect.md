On LNNet: Changing the name and updating from V2 to V3 took quite some time.
The V1 -> V2 upgrade took only 2 or 3 weeks because I was facing a series of immediate problems, but V2 -> V3 was different. For my ultimate goal of AGI—implementing autonomous reasoning in AI—I had to understand the bottlenecks of current LLMs and overcome them. I believe I have definitively overcome one of those major issues.

Writing the V2 -> V3 monograph, I thought deeply: I wanted to give AI true intelligence, not just answers based on probability. Even if my research remains humble, it feels like issuing a challenge to God Himself.
I often wondered if I'd be smiling or feeling regret when this research ends. Sometimes I think: obviously my research is small and perhaps trivial, but if it succeeds, what kind of impact would it have? ~~Of course, I suspect there will be no actual impact, remaining only as a precious experience.~~

In hindsight, if HND was about **"Can AI have intelligence?"**, then LNNet asks **"If it can have intelligence, can it go further?"**
What is certain is that LNNet at least avoids answers based on probability. That's what intelligence is. Just as we say 100 x 3 is **exactly 300**, not 99.99999% likely to be 300, I wanted that certainty.
